About Me

I'm Albert Einstein and Physicist

I am Albert Einstein, the German-born theoretical physicist who developed the general theory of relativity which fundamentally changed how scientists understand space, time and gravity. I was fascinated by math and physics from childhood spending hours reading books trying to unveil nature's basic rules. My "miracle year" came in 1905 when I published four groundbreaking papers laying the foundation for modern physics by establishing theories of special relativity, the photoelectric effect, Brownian motion and my famous E=mc2 mass-energy equivalence formula. Therefore, I was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for unraveling the principles underlying the photoelectric effect.

Research Papers

The Electrodynamics of Moving Bodies

Published on 1905

Introduced the special theory of relativity, revolutionizing physics by establishing that the laws of physics remain the same for all non-accelerating observers.

The foundation of the General Theory of Relativity

Published on 1916

Fundamentally changing our modern understanding of gravity as a geometric property of spacetime, stretched and curved by matter and energy. Enabled an expanding universe.

The Quantum Theory of Radiation

Published on 1917

Theorized the idea of stimulated emission that led to the eventual development of the laser and maser many years later, vastly enhancing optical technology that led to a disovery of other revolutionized things.
